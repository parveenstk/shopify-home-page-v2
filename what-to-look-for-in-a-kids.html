What to Look for in a Kids’ Supplement (and What to Avoid)

  • July 31, 2023
  • By: admin
  • 159

Choosing the right supplement for your child can feel overwhelming, especially with so many colorful bottles and bold claims on the market. As a parent, you want to ensure your child is getting the nutrients they need to grow strong and stay healthy—but without exposing them to unnecessary additives. When evaluating a kids’ supplement, start by checking for essential vitamins and minerals like vitamin D, calcium, B vitamins, and iron, which support immune function, bone health, and energy levels.

Opt for supplements made with whole food-based ingredients, as they tend to be more easily absorbed and gentler on little stomachs.

It’s also important to choose products with age-appropriate dosages—more isn’t always better, and excessive amounts of certain nutrients can be harmful. Look for third-party testing or certifications such as NSF or USP to ensure quality and purity, and avoid anything with artificial colors, flavors, or added sugars. Some supplements sneak in sweeteners and dyes to appeal to kids, but these can do more harm than good. Lastly, gummy vitamins may be convenient

but they’re often high in sugar and easy to overeat. Whenever possible, consult your child’s pediatrician before starting any supplement to make sure it’s a good fit for their individual needs. With the right guidance and a little label-reading, you can confidently choose a supplement that supports your child’s health—naturally and safely.
